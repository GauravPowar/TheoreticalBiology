How to make mathematical models (even at home)

July 13, 2025 Anshu Mishra, Third year BSMS 11:00 AM (IST)

Abstract: Mathematical biology takes many different forms depending on the practitioner. I take mine with one math and one biology (the so-called “big M, little b”), but others like it stronger (“little m, big B”). Under my worldview, mechanistic models are a tool to analyze biological data; a tool that infuses our knowledge of the relevant biological processes into the analytical framework. That might sound very pie-in-the-sky, and so I’ve made up an example to illustrate what I mean. The example (i have taken from the paper) has been constructed so that it doesn’t require any advanced knowledge: if you know how to add and multiply – that’s all you’ll need to understand modelling..
